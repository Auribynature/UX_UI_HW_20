Cherokee Humane Society Website Redesign

The Cherokee Humane Society website was designed to give the organization an initial online presence. We have observed that the organization’s key information isn’t easily found, which is causing lower interactions with the shelter and therefore fewer adoptions. How might we improve the current website so that visitors to the site are successful in easily finding the information they are looking for?

Objective

To gather information and insight into how people who adopt pets navigate the Cherokee Humane Society website and what they are looking for when they adopt an animal and/or donate to the society.

Hypothesis

There are many different places someone can acquire a pet from and we want to increase local awareness for pet adoption from a shelter. By redesigning the homepage and adoption page we will increase traffic to and adoptions to the Cherokee Humane Society website.

Survey Says

64.3% of those surveyed would go to their local shelters website to look for a new pet.

Interview Results

Lack of Intuitive Website Navigation Unclear Information about Charity Limited Pet Search Filters Incorrect Button Navigation

User Persona

Jenny is a stay at home mom looking to find a way to brighten up her and her family’s lives by adopting a family pet. A lot of her time is spent tending to her two children, so she would love a pet that has some previous training. Her time is limited with her home responsibilities and she so must use her time efficiently when searching.

User Journey

Jenny feels like she wants to have a new companion around her home. She feels ready to adopt a dog. She wants to adopt from a local shelter since she prefers to rescue pet or pets that are in need of a new home. She’s a novices about looking for local animals in need of a new home.

User Insight

During our user interviews, we discovered that the Cherokee Humane society has some frustrating features and disorganized information architecture. Therefore, we believe that the difficulty of finding information on their website creates limited adoptions and interactions with the shelter. We might be able to help increase pet adoptions and volunteering if we change the way the current website is structured and designed. We might do this by conducting more navigation focused usability tests, doing a card sorting activity, and performing various phases of iterating prototypes. Doing this will allow the Cherokee county humane society website to attract more traffic and therefore boost public interaction and adoptions.
